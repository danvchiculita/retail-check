Three examples of how execution, pricing and control affect real commercial outcomes in retail.

Execution

Eliminating hidden losses caused by weak in-store execution

Challenge

A supplier was facing underperforming sales, although internal reporting suggested that listing and stock levels were in place.

What we found
  • 25% of listed SKUs were physically missing from shelf, despite appearing as “in stock” in the store system
  • Planogram compliance was only 60%, with products often compressed or moved to lower shelves
  • Backroom stock was not being transferred to shelf on time due to internal replenishment issues
Actions & results
  • Operational bottlenecks in the replenishment flow were identified, and a simple rapid-alert mechanism was defined together with the client’s team
  • Store-level reaction time was significantly improved through corrective action on shelf execution
  • In parallel, bottlenecks in the supply cycle were removed by optimizing the replenishment flow
  • Execution deviations were clarified and alignment with agreed standards was restored
  • Planogram compliance returned to 95% within the first 30 days
Impact

A direct 12–15% uplift in sales was achieved simply by restoring product availability at shelf.

Strategy

Aligning price and margin before market entry

Context

The client was preparing for listing in a major retail chain, relying on theoretical assumptions regarding price positioning and category dynamics. Real-market validation was required to avoid a structurally unprofitable entry.

What we did
  • Conducted comparative shelf analysis across relevant modern trade channels
  • Mapped the price architecture of the category (entry, mid, premium) and defined the real market benchmark
  • Assessed promotional intensity through field observation and historical review of promotional catalogues over recent months
What we found
  • Promotional pressure was approximately 20% higher than initially expected
  • The planned price positioning was not aligned with the actual competitive reality in market
Actions & results
  • The reference price was adjusted by +7% to reflect real competitive dynamics
  • The client entered negotiation based on market reality rather than internal assumptions
Impact

An estimated ~15% revenue loss was avoided by correcting price positioning before listing.

Control

Protecting market share through continuous execution monitoring

Context

After the initial success of the listing, shelf performance began to deteriorate gradually. The client was at risk of losing premium positioning and negotiated shelf space.

What we monitored
  • Planogram compliance over time
  • Promotional execution
  • Product availability during peak periods
  • Competitive behavior at shelf
What we found
  • Progressive deterioration in planogram compliance
  • Replenishment delays, particularly during weekends
  • Inconsistent promotional execution in key locations
Actions & results
  • Critical execution deviations were identified and corrective mechanisms were activated at store and field-team level
  • Promotional compliance improved from 65% to 98% through focused intervention and rapid reporting
  • Product availability during critical periods was stabilized, significantly reducing the risk of Out-of-Stock
  • Shelf execution was stabilized in higher-risk locations
Impact

Consistent shelf visibility was maintained, preventing shopper migration to competing brands caused by lack of availability.
